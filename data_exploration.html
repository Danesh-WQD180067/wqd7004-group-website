Data Exploration

This page explores the global trends of Socioeconomic Status and Economic Freedoms

Economic Freedom by Countries charts

  • Economic Freedom of 138 countries for the year 1970, 1980, 1990, 2000 and 2010.
  • It shows the trend of the degree of economic freedom by country.
options(repr.plot.width=8, repr.plot.height=10)
country_dataset <- read.csv("data/imp_ses_efw.csv")
country_dataset %>% filter(!is.na(ECONOMIC_FREEDOM) | !is.na(year)) %>% 
  ggplot(aes(year, ECONOMIC_FREEDOM, fill = ECONOMIC_FREEDOM, group = 1))+
  geom_line()+
  scale_color_viridis(option = "plasma",direction = -1, discrete=FALSE) +
  facet_wrap(~country)+theme_bw()+
  theme(legend.position = "none", axis.text.x = element_blank(), 
        strip.text.x = element_text(size = 6))+
  xlab(" ") + ylab("")+ ggtitle("ECONOMIC FREEDOM INDEX OF THE WORLD 1970, 1980, 1990, 2000 and 2010")

Findings:

For the past 5 decades,

  1. North America has remained the highest scoring region in terms of economic freedom.
  2. Before the 21st century, South American continent’s economic freedom were on the lower end and it improved later on.
  3. Ecocomic Freedom Index in Africa remained unsupportive since 1970.
  4. In the european region, western Europe have shown robust improvements, while eastern Europe have seen slower improvements.
  5. In Asia Pacific, economic freedom index were mixed.
